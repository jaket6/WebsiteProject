EE 323 Teaching Assistant
January 14, 2014 - May 21, 2014



My duties included the preparation of materials which were necessary to each lab session along with having a working knowledge to the academic material that would be used in lab. I was expected to have a strong understanding in the presented subject matter in order to assist students in completing the lab exercise. I was also responsible for collecting and grading each individual lab report within a week of being turned in.

This particular class (EE323) required me to have extensive knowledge in the following concepts:

- PSPICE simulation software
- Operational Amplifier basics, explorations, experimentation and interpretation of inverting and non-inverting amplifiers
- Voltage and current offsets, miller integrator, frequency effects
- Junction diodes basics, diode action, rectifying and filtering, large and small signal models
- MOSFET measurements, conductivity parameter, transconductors, CMOS active load amplifiers
- Bipolar transistor basics, basic junction currents and biasing schemes, amplifiers voltage gain, and input impedances, large regional distortion

Along with a conceptual understanding of the material, it was important that i could communicate the expectations of the lab paired with the desired result of each experiment. Exceptional communication skills were required to effectively interact with each student and help them settle any unresolved problems.

Other typical skills/traits were necessary to carry out the job such as:



- Experience with Microsoft office and Excel programs to keep grades
- Promptness to arrive at labs and scheduled appointments early each week
- Reliability to grade and prepare for each lab weekly
- Honesty to grade fairly and indiscriminately based solely on content
- Patience to deal with students of different learning speeds
